Section 1

Introduction

inblock.io assets GmbH ("inblock.io," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our siwx-oidc authentication service and related services (collectively, the "Service").

siwx-oidc is a CAIP-122 to OpenID Connect bridge. It allows you to sign in using a blockchain wallet (such as an Ethereum wallet) or a WebAuthn passkey and receive standard OIDC tokens in return. Any OIDC relying party can use it as an identity provider.

As a German company based in the European Union, we process your personal data in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") as our primary framework. We also respect the privacy rights of users in other jurisdictions, including the United States.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with the terms of this Privacy Policy, please do not access or use the Service.

Section 2

What this Privacy Policy Covers

This Privacy Policy covers the processing of personal data that we collect when you:

  • Visit or interact with the siwx-oidc authentication service;
  • Authenticate using a blockchain wallet or WebAuthn passkey;
  • Use the device authorization flow (e.g., QR code login);
  • Link a passkey to an existing wallet identity;
  • Interact with any relying party application that uses siwx-oidc as its identity provider.

This Privacy Policy does not apply to the practices of third parties that we do not own or control, including relying party applications that integrate with siwx-oidc. When you authenticate through siwx-oidc to access a third-party application, that application's own privacy policy governs how it handles your data after authentication.

3.2 Categories of Sources

We collect personal data from the following categories of sources:

  • Directly from you: When you authenticate with a wallet or passkey, you provide your identity information and credentials directly through the sign-in process. When you link a passkey to a wallet, you initiate that connection.
  • Public records: Blockchain addresses, DIDs, and ENS domain names are derived from publicly available blockchain data. We may resolve ENS names to provide a human-readable display name in your OIDC profile.
  • Automated collection: Session cookies and tokens are generated automatically as part of the authentication flow. These are essential for the service to function.

3.3 Purposes for Collection

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Authentication: Verifying your identity through wallet signatures (CAIP-122) or WebAuthn passkey ceremonies, and issuing OIDC tokens (ID tokens, access tokens, refresh tokens) to relying parties.
  • Session management: Maintaining authentication state, managing authorization codes, and handling token refresh operations.
  • Device provisioning: When used with a Matrix homeserver (MSC3861), provisioning and managing user devices on the homeserver.
  • Account management: Enabling account-related actions such as cross-signing key resets and passkey linking.
  • Security: Detecting and preventing unauthorized access, fraud, and other malicious activity.
  • Service improvement: Diagnosing technical issues, monitoring service health, and improving reliability.
  • Legal compliance: Complying with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes.

Section 5

How We Share Your Personal Data

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your personal data with the following categories of recipients:

  • Relying parties you authorize: When you authenticate through siwx-oidc, the relying party application you are logging into receives your DID (and optionally your ENS name) as part of the OIDC token claims. You initiate this sharing each time you choose to sign in to a relying party. The specific data shared is limited to your identity (the "sub" claim) and the scopes requested during authentication.
  • Matrix homeserver (when applicable): If siwx-oidc is configured as the OIDC provider for a Matrix homeserver (via MSC3861), we share your DID and device information with the homeserver for user and device provisioning. The homeserver is operated under its own data processing terms.
  • Infrastructure and service providers: We use hosting and infrastructure providers (such as cloud hosting, Redis for session storage) to operate the Service. These providers process data on our behalf under data processing agreements that comply with GDPR Art. 28.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose your personal data if required to do so by law, or in good faith belief that such disclosure is necessary to comply with a legal obligation, protect and defend our rights or property, prevent fraud, or protect the safety of users or the public.

We do not share your data with advertising networks, data brokers, or analytics providers.

Section 6

Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use only essential cookies that are strictly necessary for the authentication service to function. We do not use tracking cookies, analytics cookies, advertising cookies, or any third-party cookies.

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
siwx Essential (first-party) Carries the signed CAIP-122 message and cryptographic signature from the wallet sign-in step to the server-side verification step. Required for the authentication flow to complete. 48 hours (or until the CAIP-122 message expires)

We do not use web beacons, tracking pixels, fingerprinting scripts, or any other similar tracking technologies. Server-side session data (session tokens, authorization codes, device codes) is stored in our Redis database, not in browser cookies.

Because we use only strictly necessary cookies, consent under Art. 5(3) of the ePrivacy Directive is not required. You may configure your browser to refuse cookies, but doing so will prevent you from authenticating through the Service.

Section 7

Data Security and Retention

7.1 Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • All OIDC tokens are signed using ES256 (ECDSA with P-256) cryptographic keys.
  • All communication with the Service is encrypted in transit using TLS.
  • Session data is stored in a secured Redis instance with access controls.
  • WebAuthn ceremonies use challenge-response protocols with short-lived challenges (120 seconds) to prevent replay attacks.
  • We never store your private keys, wallet seed phrases, or passkey private keys. Only public keys and credential identifiers are stored.
  • Authorization codes are single-use and consumed immediately upon token issuance.
  • Refresh tokens are rotated on each use (the old token is deleted when a new one is issued).

7.2 Retention Periods

Data Type Retention Period Notes
Session tokens 300 seconds (5 minutes) Automatically deleted on expiry
Authorization codes 300 seconds (5 minutes) Single-use; consumed on token exchange
WebAuthn challenges 120 seconds (2 minutes) Automatically deleted on expiry or use
Device codes 1800 seconds (30 minutes) Automatically deleted on expiry or approval
Access tokens 300 seconds (5 minutes) Short-lived by design
Refresh tokens 86,400 seconds (24 hours) Rotated on each use; old tokens deleted
Passkey credentials Until deletion is requested Retained to enable passkey authentication
Account linking data Until deletion is requested Retained to map passkeys to wallet DIDs
CAIP-122 signed messages Not stored beyond the session Verified and discarded after authentication

Blockchain wallet addresses and DIDs that appear on public blockchains remain publicly available regardless of any action we take. Our deletion of your data from our systems does not affect public blockchain records.

Section 8

Your Rights Under GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under the GDPR with respect to your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15): You have the right to request confirmation of whether we process your personal data, and if so, to access that data along with information about the purposes, categories, recipients, retention periods, and your rights.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16): You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal data or completion of incomplete personal data. Note that blockchain-derived data (wallet addresses, DIDs) is determined by cryptographic keys and cannot be "corrected" in the traditional sense.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17): You have the right to request deletion of your personal data when it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, when you withdraw consent, or when there is no overriding legitimate ground for processing. We can delete passkey credentials, account linking data, and any session data from our systems. We cannot delete data that exists on public blockchains.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20): You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit that data to another controller. Your DID is inherently portable as it is derived from your own cryptographic keys.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18): You have the right to request restriction of processing in certain circumstances, such as when you contest the accuracy of data or when you have objected to processing pending verification.
  • Right to object (Art. 21): You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interest. We will cease processing unless we demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, rights, and freedoms.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)): Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw your consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing performed before the withdrawal.
  • Right to lodge a complaint (Art. 77): You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. Because inblock.io assets GmbH is located in Weimar, Thuringia, Germany, the competent supervisory authority is:

    Thuringian State Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information (TLfDI)
    Haeusserstrasse 8, 99096 Erfurt, Germany
    Website: www.tlfdi.de

    You may also lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority of your place of habitual residence or place of work.

How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of the above rights, please send a request to hello@inblock.io. We will respond to your request within 30 days, as required by the GDPR.

For identity verification purposes: if your identity is associated with a blockchain wallet, we may ask you to prove ownership by signing a challenge message with the relevant cryptographic key. For passkey-based identities, we may ask you to complete a WebAuthn authentication ceremony. This ensures that only the rightful owner of an identity can exercise rights over the associated data.

We do not charge a fee for exercising your rights, except in cases of manifestly unfounded or excessive requests, where we may charge a reasonable fee or refuse to act.
